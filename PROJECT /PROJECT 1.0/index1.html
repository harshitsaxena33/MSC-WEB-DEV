Delhi power bills up with hike in purchase surcharge

NEW DELHI: Delhiites who consume over 200 units of electricity in a month will have to pay higher bills after a hike in the power purchase adjustment cost between 1.5% and 9% announced on Monday. Delhi is supplied power by four discoms - BSES Yamuna Power Limited, BSES Rajdhani Power Limited, Tata Power Delhi Distribution Limited and NDMC. The hike will vary for consumers in different parts of the city as the increase in PPAC is different for each discom.

Refrigerator prices may rise up to 5% as revised BEE labelling norms come into force

Prices of refrigerators are likely to increase by up to 5 per cent as the revised norms of the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) have become applicable from January 1 this year. According to manufacturers like Godrej Appliances, Haier and Panasonic, implementation of the new norms may put an additional burden of 2-5 per cent on consumers, depending on the models. Besides tightening the labelling, the new norms also mandate separate star labelling for freezers and refrigerator provisionin provisioning units (storage part) of the frost-free models. "Now, we have to declare labelling for both. That is the new change," said Godrej Appliances Business Head and Executive Vice President Kamal Nandi told PTI.

Amid technical glitches, 1.61 lakh sign up for free electricity scheme in two days

Technical glitches marred registrations for the Karnataka government’s Gruha Jyothi scheme, providing free electricity up to 200 units a month, as it was opened to consumers Sunday. In the first 2 days, 1,61,958 consumers have signed up online for the scheme in the state, as per Bangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited
